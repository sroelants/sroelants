Why Sorted?

Sorting algorithms are both the basis as well as the bread and butter of algorithm design. They are the first class of algorithms any computer science student studies. Because most basic sorting algorithms have a fairly straightforward implementation, they are perfect for getting used to reasoning about time and space complexity. Also, countless more complicated problems have solutions that depend, at their core, on whether or not we can sort something. And, as a consequence, their complexity will be bounded from below by how efficiently we can sort a list of numbers. Deeply understanding these sorting algorithms, then, is a crucial step in learning to reason about complexity and more sophisticated problems.

But it can be hard, especially at first, to translate these abstract recipes into something tangible. Sorted lets you play with different sorting algorithms to see how they perform on different sizes of arrays.
